About Me

I earned my Ph.D. in Bioengineering in South Korea and am currently seeking postdoctoral opportunities in space neuroscience. My doctoral work focused on blood-brain barrier dysfunction and macrophage-driven inflammation following traumatic brain injury (TBI). I'm transitioning into space bioscience, particularly exploring how the brain responds to long-term exposure to microgravity and space radiation.

Research

I established a TBI mouse model in a newly built lab, managed animal experiments across two campuses, and discovered the anti-inflammatory effect of intravenous recombinant protein X. Recent research includes RNA-seq analysis of NASA GeneLab datasets to study ferroptosis-related gene changes in space-exposed mouse brains, supporting the hypothesis that long-term spaceflight may induce astrocyte vulnerability.

Ideas

I aim to investigate the intersection of spaceflight stressors and neurovascular regulation. Specific interests include astrocyte ferroptosis under cosmic radiation, transcriptomic shifts in brain immune cells during prolonged microgravity, and adaptation failure in hippocampal regions post-flight.
