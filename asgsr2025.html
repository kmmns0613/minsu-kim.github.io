‚Ä¢ Analysis Framework

1. Dataset

I analyzed long-duration spaceflight RNA-seq datasets from the NASA GeneLab OSDR (GSE239336). Normalized expression matrices and differential expression (DEG) results from each brain region were used to identify transcriptional changes induced by spaceflight.

2. Gene Set Enrichment Score (GSEA / ssGSEA)

To determine which biological pathways were altered by spaceflight, I performed both group-level fast GSEA and sample-level ssGSEA/GSVA using KEGG, Reactome, GO, Hallmark, and FerrDb V2 gene sets. This dual-level approach allowed me to identify consistent pathway shifts and extract the leading-edge genes driving those changes.

3. Hedge‚Äôs g (Effect Size Analysis)

Ferroptosis involves multiple mechanistic axes, including iron homeostasis, ROS generation, lipid peroxidation, and mitochondrial function. Using representative gene groups for each axis, I calculated Hedge‚Äôs g to quantify which mechanisms were most strongly affected by spaceflight. This provided a measure of biological impact that goes beyond simple statistical significance.

4. Pathway Correlation

To determine whether pathways activated by spaceflight and suppressed by antioxidant pretreatment (BuOE) are truly linked to spaceflight-induced ferroptosis, I analyzed correlations among ssGSEA-derived pathway scores. Spearman correlation was used to identify coordinated upward or downward shifts across pathways, and Hotelling‚Äôs test was applied to evaluate pathway-level separation between the GC-SAL and FLT-SAL groups.

5. Scoring System

Finally, I constructed a scoring framework integrating leading-edge gene frequency, pathway consistency, effect sizes (Hedge‚Äôs g), and FerrDb annotations to identify high-confidence molecular targets of spaceflight-induced ferroptosis. The detailed scoring logic is provided in the Code Snippets section.
